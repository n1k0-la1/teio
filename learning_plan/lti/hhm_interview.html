II. Interview questions ::>-<::

  • To start us off, can you introduce yourself a little? How'd you get into this field?
  • I remember you saying that you used to be a professor in exhibit design before; can you tell me a little about your experiences there?
  • What other places that you've worked at do you feel have been the most impactful?
  • How do the experiences you've had there've helped you with your career? What skills and expertise have you built that you feel is the most helpful for you?
  • Are there any skills you've learned outside of your career that you feel are vital?
  • Why did you end up deciding to join the HHM?
  • What was the most exciting parts when you first joined? What about now? ( <- filler question tbh)
  • Did you have another position here before you became director?
  • How're the positions you've had before different to the one you have now?
  • What's your usual day-to-day like at the HHM?
  • What's your favorite part of your job? What's more difficult?
  • Looking back, would you do anything differently if you had to start from scratch again?
  • Do you feel like the environment of the field is different from when you first started? How so?
  • Do you feel like it's harder to enter now than before?
  • What experience/education did you have coming in, and what experience/education do you feel people should have coming in now?
  • Ah, I wouldn't want to cut into your time so we should start wrapping up; do you know anyone else in the field who'd be interested in doing an interview? Could I please have their contact information?

IV. Reflection o(￣┰￣*)ゞ

Reflect on your preparation for the interview: Did your questions help you learn more about the person and your interest?

  • Which questions were the best? Why?

    Uhmm... All of them were pretty good? Lol? And this time I don't mean "All of my questions were the same amount of bad", prommy. I think the question that provided the best unexpected results though was my first one; "How'd you get into this field?", because she talked a lot about her experience growing up in Mexico City, (which is the city with one of the most museums in the world ?!); and through that I got to ask more interesting questions about her relationship to museums there, etc.

  • Which questions do you need to get rid of or reword? Why?

    It's not that I needed to reword it necessarily... Just that "What's your usual day-to-day like" didn't really get the response I expected it would, so I had to reword it and ask again; but you know.. Whatever. It's a non-issue.

  • What are you most proud of from the experience? Why?

    I think I did really good, especially for my first interview! I only really needed to look at my notes/questions once in the beginning, and I feel like the rest of the interview was pretty smooth sailing! No pausing to think or stumbling over questions from me! Again, like with Chelsea, I feel like she talked most of the time (Which is what I want); but this time it felt less like she was 'carrying the interview'. Nancy complemented me on being very articulate and said I could use the station if I wanted to broadcast any of my interviews in the future! :D

  • What can you improve on for your next interview experience? Who can help with this?

    Uhm... I think I should probably read over my questions a couple more times on my way there, which is something I need to do... And I also need to not be so out of shape that I feel winded from walking to the bus stop... That's also a me problem...
