II. Interview questions ::>-<::

  • To start us off, can you introduce yourself a little? How'd you get into this field?
  • What education did you get? What undergrad/postgrad degree?
  • How was your experience doing that?
  • Have you worked in archives anywhere else?
  • What other places that you've worked at do you feel have been the most impactful?
  • How do the experiences you've had there've helped you with your career? What skills and expertise have you built that you feel is the most helpful for you?
  • How did you end up working at the Seattle Art Museum?
  • Did you have another position here before you became an Archivist?
  • How did you end up as acting Librarian?
  • How're the positions you've had before different to the one you have now?
  • What's your usual day-to-day like at the museum?
  • How're your responsibilities as an Archivist different from the onces you're taking up noe as a Librarian?
  • Do you feel like the environment of the field is different from when you first started? How so?
  • Do you feel like it's harder to enter now than before?
  • What experience/education did you have coming in, and what experience/education do you feel people should have coming in now?
  • Do you know anyone else in the field who'd be interested in doing an interview?/Would any Archivists/Curators/Librarians working here be interested in doing an interview? Could I please have their contact information?
