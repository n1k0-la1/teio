"Draw boxes (correctly) to improve your art" Youtube, uploaded by pikat, 17, Jan. 2024

starting point for boxes and cylinders and such. 4 points -> connect edges -> make x in the middle to find center -> extend from middle to section into quarters -> divide 2 sides diagonally -> find intersection with center point -> drop line -> do same on other side -> boom sectioned into thirds. to do cylinder arch in each quarter

Shouh, Pete. Tutorial and exercise on box rotation. 26 Jan. 2022.

used this as a ref to check my own work against. look here to see my notes on what I learned

"Go from drawing BOXES to BODIES" Youtube, uploaded by
Kimon Nicolaïdes. The Natural Way to Draw. London, Souvenir, 2008.

used this as a primer on life drawing/figure drawing instead of fumbling around blindly like normal. it's pretty comphrehensive & i didn't read it cover to cover, instead I read the intro and basic bits, and will page pack to anything I have difficulty with. It has a schedule for figure drawing which i hope to adapt into something I can do over the summer; (it's a little daunting as it is now) and the main points I took from it were 'gesture' and 'contour' drawing, and the basics of each

Bayles, David, and Ted Orland. Art & Fear : Observations on the Perils (and Rewards) of Artmaking. Santa Cruz, Ca, Image Continuum Press ; Saint Paul, Mn, 1993.

describes my art making complex to a t, almost. You could even say I was read like a book. Don't though. This was really comforting to hear others also describe my issues, and it helped me stop dragging my feet on filling up the sketchbook. Stopping, starting, and stopping again on a piece is fine. Restarting is a natural part of the cycle of creation. Making 'mundane' art is hard. It's fine. Quanity over quality, and such.
